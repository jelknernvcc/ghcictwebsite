CSC 205: Computer Organization: Week 3

Week of Monday, September 13, 2021

Overview

Last week we shared presentations on logic gates. This week we will take a deeper dive into the ideas and history that led to the development of logic gates and their use in computers.

Tasks

After briefly discussing the topics below, we will form pairs to investigate and present these topics at our next Thursday, September 16th.

  • The history of Boolean algebra and logic circuits (Jack and Lucas).
  • An introduction to Boolean algebra and digital logic, including the algebraic rules such as DeMorgan's laws that govern it (Eduardo and Amira).
  • Karnaugh maps and their use in designing digital circuits (Rachael and Chris).
  • Flip-flops and their use as memory together with a 4-bit adder circuit. (Leila)

After briefly introducing these topics, we will assign them to pairs and get right to work investigating them.

Friday, September 17th

Tasks

  • Share presentations on history of boolean algebra and logic circuits, intro to boolean algebra and digital logic (Francesco and Oscar), Karnaugh maps and flip-flops and a 4-bit adder.
  • Discuss number systems in general and binary, octal, and hexadecimal in particular.
