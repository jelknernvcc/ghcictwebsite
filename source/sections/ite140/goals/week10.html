Overview

This week we will begin using PostgreSQL to create and query tables.

Goals for Week 10

This week we will:

  • share our presentations on the vocubularly from the Lift Off chapter assigned last week.
  • access our shared database server and begin experimenting with postgres.
  • begin working our way through the Transit chapter in our text.

I'll ask for links to your vocabularly presentations, and then use those as resources to develop a vocabulary quiz. This vocabularly is important for to us to develop a broad understanding of the database landscape.

Accessing Our Database Servers

As I've mentioned many a time this year, we are pioneers, explores in a new course at ACC, navigating uncharted curricular territory. I'm not sure the best way to provide us with database access, so we will start out with two database servers, one inside our classroom and one online, to which you will have different access.

We will connect to both of these servers during class this week.

Reading the Transit Chapter with a Goal Toward Learning

Using our text effectively requires an approach with which many of us may not be familiar. The book is really strong on context, but equally sparse on details. Filling in those details is left to us.

On Friday we will talk about how to do this and follow an example experiment I developed in my git repo notes for this chapter.

Homework

Our homework this week will be to read the next chapter, Transit from our book, taking notes as we go. Please read (and take notes) with the following goals in mind:

  • Write down any new vocabulary with which you are unfamiliar.
  • Note any skills and concepts (practice and theory) which may require additional investigation.
